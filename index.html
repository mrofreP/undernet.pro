why undernet

Everything chat should be — and has been since 1994.

Undernet pairs the openness of classic IRC with the services that keep communities safe and welcoming — no ads, no tracking, no algorithm. Just real people, in real time, on a network built and run by the people who use it.

&nowhere

No account, no algorithm

Pick a nickname and you're chatting — no sign-up, no profile, no ads. Nothing ranks or hides what you read: you see every message, in the order it was sent.

/connect

Chat your way

IRC is an open standard, so the client is your call — chat straight from the browser, or bring mIRC, HexChat, Textual, WeeChat, irssi or a mobile app. One network, wherever you like it.

~40 servers

Always on, worldwide

Around forty volunteer-run servers span the Americas, Europe and beyond, all speaking ircu — the server software Undernet wrote and has spent decades refining to stay fast and ride out netsplits.

#help

Newcomers always welcome

Never touched IRC? You're in good company. Ask anything in #help, or join OpSchool to learn the ropes of running a channel. Undernet has been showing newcomers around since 1994.

#usernames

Run by its community

Undernet is a volunteer project, steered by open committees — Routing, Channel Service, Coder and User — and shaped by the people who log in every day. It answers to its community, not shareholders.

#cservice

Your channel, protected

Register your channel with X, Undernet's Channel Service, and set ops, bans and access lists exactly how you want them. CService guards your community's home — even while every op is asleep.
